In your first meeting with the PE firm you are presented with some facts about the business. What issues do you identify?

Answer 1

Basic Points

  • Revenue growth 30% per year
  • Online and catalogue segments appear to be growing the fastest (50% 2009-2010), while In Store revenue is growing at less than 10%
  • Need to understand profitability of the different channels to assess performance

Advanced Comments

  • Stores: Revenue per store: $0.5m in 2009, $0.5m in 2010. Per Store revenue is flat, so growth is only being delivered by store growth.

  • Catalogues: Revenue per Catalogue: $50 in 2009, $100 in 2010. Catalogue distribution has diminished, but per catalogue revenue has grown 100%. This suggests that less profitable catalogue customers are being transitioned to other channels.

  • Online Retail: Revenue per Online Member: $50 in 2009, $30 in 2010. Online Users have grown, but revenue per user has declined. This suggests that new users are less profitable, perhaps transitioning from the catalogue channel.

  • Expectations: If PE firm aquired Zobe for $150M last year, this implies a revenue multiple of close to 1x and an EBITDA multiple of approx 20x - a very high valuation. Therefore PE firm is expecting significant growth.

  • Context: Difficult to assess current performance and prospects without knowing anything about the market - overall growth of the niche sportswear/lifestyle sector, competition etc
