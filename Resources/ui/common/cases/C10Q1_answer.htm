Chelmwest holds a portfolio of 50k residential mortgages. How is this performing? How could it be improved?

  • Product: 2 year floating interest at base rate with £1k set up fee, defaults to base rate + 10%
  • Adoption: 50k mortgages in portfolio, base rate 10%, average outstanding balance £100k, 10 year total mortgage term
  • Churn: 80% of the mortgage portfolio turns over after 2 years
  • Costs: Set up £500, annual servicing £500
  • Capital available: £6bn
  • Competitors: National banks offering fixed rate 2 year products, other regional banks going online
  • Customers: Younger buyers with average income £40k switch often, older buyers hold

Answer 1

Basic Points

  • Revenue = £620M comprising Setup Fees (40%x50kx£1k = £20M), 2 year interest (80%x50kx£100kx10% = £400M), Other Interest (20%x50kx£100kx20% = £200M)
  • Costs = £35M comprising Setup (40%x50kx£500 = £10M), Annual Servicing (50kx£500 = £25M)
  • Profit = £585M
  • Most revenue is from interest payments. Can ChelmWest get more people to stay after 2 years?
  • Costs seem relatively low. Have we considered bad debt and opportunity costs?

Advanced Comments

  • ROI: Mortgage business is highly profitable, but also need to think of the Cost of Capital. What else could ChelmWest be doing with this money? With capital employed of $5bn, ChelmWest make approx 12% return on investment. Is this high enough? Only approx 2% greater than base rate of 10%
  • Market Share: At 50k mortgages, ChelmWest holds only about 1% of total UK mortgage market (assuming 10m households). Plenty of opportunity to grow
  • Competition: Is it realistic to expect more people to stay after 2 years? For young buyers interest payments would be half their income. This may not be a source of growth
  • Vertical Integration: Most mortgage lenders do not hold a portfolio of loans. Instead they sell packages onto the secondary market which allows for more mortgage origination. Can we do this?
  • Risks: Risk is currently pretty low for ChelmWest as they offer floating rate mortgages only. Opportunity to increase Loan to Value ratio and attract more customers
