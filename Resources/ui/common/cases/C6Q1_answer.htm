Szmu Pharma is deciding whether to proceed with Phase 3 of clinical trials for a new Asthma drug. So far £3bn has been spent on Phase 1 & 2. Estimate that Phase 3 will cost £3.4bn, take 4 years and have an 80% chance of success. Should the company proceed?

  • Estimate that this drug will be 100% more effective than other treatments
  • Equivalents priced at $1k/year/patient
  • Manufacturing and distribution will cost approx $500 for 1 years supply of the new drug
  • Initial launch in UK and USA where 1 in 10 children below age 20 have asthma
  • Estimate that competitor will launch an even more effective drug in 6 years

Answer 1

Basic Points

  • Estimate market size 9M patients (population 60M in UK, 300M in USA, ave life expectancy 80 years and assume even distribution of population across age groups)
  • Initially might take time to get people to switch, so say 4.5M in Year 1, 9M in Year 2
  • Potential profit per year $2.25bn in Year 1, $4.5bn in Year 2 = Total Profit of $6.75bn
  • Payoff in 1 - 2 years
  • Phase 1 and 2 are a sunk cost so they do not factor into the decision

Advanced Comments

  • Outcomes: Although there is an 80% chance of success there are effectively 2 discrete outcomes - succeed and make $6.75bn or fail and lose $3.4bn

  • Risks: Delay in clinical trials causes product to be late to market. Sales and marketing not effective at persuading people to switch medication
