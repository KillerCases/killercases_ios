How should Chelmwest conduct marketing and distribution if it is to expand it's mortgage portfolio?

  • Current customer base is heavily focused around West Midlands with traditional branch presence
  • ChelmWest is considering 2 modes of marketing and distribution: New branches or online marketing
  • New branch will cost £1M to setup, £50k/yr to run and serve 500 customers (i.e. £100/yr/customer)
  • Online marketing will cost approx £2k to acquire a new customer, £50/year to service
  • Retention is 10 years for a branch customer, 2 years for online customer

Answer 3

Basic Points

  • Branches: Lifetime cost of a customer = (£1M + (£50k/yrx10))/500/10 = £2.1k/customer
  • Online: Lifetime cost of a customer = £2k + £50/yr x 2 = £2.1k/customer
  • Costs appear to be very similar
  • However, we have made a lot of assumptions for the branch model - annual costs, retention?
  • Online probably offers more customers and requires less investment upfront

Advanced Comments

  • What is key selling point?: Customer service, trust worthy institution, member based organization? If so, branch model may be a good fit. However, if expect to attract customers based on low fees, high LTV ratio etc then online may be a better channel.

  • What are growth aspirations of this company? : If ChelmWest is aiming for quick growth then online may be the best option, despite high churn of customers. If ChelmWest is seeking a stable and loyal customer base then branches may be the best option.

  • Cannibalization: Branches and online banking are likely to reach different audiences. In addition it is unlikely that a profitable branch customer will become a less profitable online customer if ChelmWest offers both channels. Therefore ChelmWest might as well offer both options.
