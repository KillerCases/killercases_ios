Banana Book is deciding how to price their new Banana Book Lite, a personal laptop computer which would come with the Banana operating system. The existing Banana Book Pro laptop is prived $1.5k. Should the Banana Book Lite be priced at $500 or $1000?



Potential Price Market Share
BB Pro $1.5k 5%
Other PCs under $1k 20%
Other PCs under $500 75%
  • Cost/Laptop: Materials $200, Labour $100, Distribution $100, Other $100
  • Product features: Attractively designed hardware, fast and slick user experience
  • Software: Price $500/package, Development costs $10M, Other Costs minimal
  • Compatibility: Banana Books can only run the Banana software. Other personal computers can install and run any type of software including Banana products

Answer 1

Basic Points

  • Total computer ownership in US approx 150M (50% of 300M population). This conclusion can be reached by assuming an even population distribution across age groups with the following laptop ownership: 0-20 yrs 20% own a laptop, 20-40 yrs 80%, 40-60 yrs 80%, 60+ yrs 20%.
  • Assume people replace a laptop every 3 years
  • Number of laptops purchased each year 50M
  • Scenario (a) Price $500, No Profit, Max Vol 37.5M
  • Scenario (b) Price $1K, Revenue $10BN (10M x $1k), Costs $5BM, Profit $5BN

Advanced Comments

  • Software: Breakeven volume for software is 20k units (Fixed Costs $10M / Gross Margin $500). This is a high fixed cost business but production and distribution is very cheap. As a result, there is a lot of profit to be made from selling software packages to Banana Book Lite owners who cannot operate other software on their devices.

  • Overall Profit: Scenario (a) make an additional $18.75B from Software. Scenario (b) make only $5B from software. Therefore Scenario (a) is more profitable overall.

  • Razor Blade: This is the typical Razor Blade scenario. Banana Book should sell the Banana Book Lite product at breakeven in order to make more money on software.

  • Other opportunities: Also potential to make money on selling high margin accessories. Just another reason to go for the $500 price point.

  • Pricing Dynamics: If the current Banana Book Pro is $1.5k then what will people think when a Lite version is introduced for less? What is the real price elasticity? Is there a risk of cannibalizing other sales? Do people perceive that this is a more "closed system" than some others which will require more expenditure on software?
