After their strategic review, Food Aid decides to focus on logistics and food aid. How should they measure success?

Answer 3

Basic Points

  1. Volume: No of people who receive food aid per year
  2. Performance: Speed of response
  3. Efficiency: Cost per person who receives aid
  4. Strategic Goals: Reduction in short term hunger needs

Advanced Comments

  1. Importance of Metrics: In For Profit Organizations success can easily be measured in terms of Revenue, Profit and Shareholder Value (like Return on Investment). By contrast, this is a much bigger challenge for Non-Profit Organizations. However, metrics can help to focus an organization, demonstrate accountability and attract donor funding.

  2. Risk of Unintended Consequences: With any metric or performance goal, there is always a risk that behavior will be distorted in unintended ways, in an effort to achieve the goal or improve a metric. For example, focus on speed of response might lead to a lack of attention on quality or cost of provisions.
