What is the market size for fast casual dining in London?

  • London population 7.5M
  • 1/3rd of people age 20-60 buy lunch out
  • People buy lunch 3x per week on average
  • Average price of a meal £5
  • Restaurants will be open for breakfast and lunch serving parathas, yogurts, daal and biryani

Answer 1

Basic Points

  • No of meals = 3.75M (population assuming equal age distribution 0 - 80 years) x 1/3 (buying out) x 3 (meals per week purchased) x 50 (wks of the year) = 187.5M (200M approx)
  • Market Size = 200M meals x £5 = £1.0B
  • Growth opportunities include more meals eaten out, more affordable options, growth in the population, higher prices

Advanced Comments

  • Target Market: May be helpful to narrow down the range to a tighter audience of target customers e.g. business people earning >£50k working in Central London. This makes it easier to conceptualize the likely market.

  • Market Share: Market size is interesting, but not hugely helpful for Biryani Brunch. Unlikely that they will ever capture more than a very small share of this market is highly fragmented and competition is intense

  • Location: In local restaurant markets there are factors which are more powerful that the theoretical market size e.g. location, nearby competition, popularity of concept etc

  • Funding: Biryani brunch has limited capital available to invest in this venture (their own savings, cashflow and bank loans). This will limit the pace of growth in the short term.
