Definition: Outsourcing is the contracting out of a business function - commonly one previously performed in-house - to an external provider.

The decision involves a trade off between cost and efficiency on one hand, and control of quality or intellectual property on the other hand. If a function is considered to be core of the value of the product/service on offer then outsourcing is unlikely.

E.g. Unlikely to outsource Research and Development for silicon chip design. However, if the function is peripheral to the main product/service then outsourcing is more likely e.g. After sales support for Personal Computers.
