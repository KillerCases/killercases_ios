Definition: Consolidation is the number of different players in an industry. If there are only a few players then consolidation is high. If there are many players then fragmentation is high.

Over a period of time, industries typically become more consolidated and less fragmented. However, the degree of consolidation will always depend on structural factors of competitiveness.

e.g. Restaurant industry is relatively fragmented and competitive because it is hard to establish barriers to entry. By comparison energy generation is highly consolidated because there are scale advantages in production.
